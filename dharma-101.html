About the program

Discover the wisdom of Sanatana Dharma.

Dharma 101 is an informational, discussion-based program designed to help participants understand the meaning of Dharma, explore core Hindu philosophical ideas, and appreciate the rich diversity of Hindu thought and worship. No prior knowledge is required.

Course details

12-week program curriculum

Each 60-minute session includes an opening reflection, main teaching and presentation, and group discussion. The syllabus is designed as a guide and may evolve based on the needs and flow of the group.

Week 1

What Is Dharma?

Introduction to Hindu Dharma and its worldview
  • Meaning of "Dharma"
  • Hinduism vs. Sanatana Dharma
  • Overview of Hindu history and civilization
  • Core ideas: karma, rebirth, moksha, duty, and harmony
  • Diversity within Hindu traditions
Week 2

The Vedas & Sacred Knowledge

Understanding Hindu scriptures
  • What are the Vedas?
  • Shruti and Smriti
  • Upanishads and philosophical inquiry
  • Epics and Puranas
  • Oral tradition and memorization
Week 3

The Divine in Hindu Dharma

Understanding God, gods, and ultimate reality
  • Brahman and Atman
  • One reality, many forms
  • Trimurti: Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva
  • Devi and the Divine Feminine
  • Is Hindu Dharma polytheistic, monotheistic, or something else?
Week 4

Karma, Rebirth & Liberation

The Hindu understanding of life and destiny
  • Law of karma
  • Reincarnation and samsara
  • Paths toward moksha
  • Free will and responsibility
  • Ethics and consequences
Week 5

Yoga Beyond Exercise

The spiritual meaning of yoga
  • Meaning of yoga
  • Four paths: Karma, Bhakti, Jnana, and Raja Yoga
  • Meditation and discipline
  • Yoga in modern society
Week 6

Daily Practice & Puja

How Hindu Dharma is practiced in everyday life
  • Puja and ritual worship
  • Temple and home traditions
  • Aarti, mantra, and prayer
  • Importance of intention and devotion
  • Sacred symbols and offerings
Week 7

The Bhagavad Gita & Life Guidance

Lessons from the Bhagavad Gita
  • Context of the Bhagavad Gita
  • Arjuna's dilemma
  • Duty and inner conflict
  • Selfless action
  • Leadership and character
Week 8

Festivals & Celebrations

Understanding Hindu festivals and sacred time
  • Diwali, Holi, and Navaratri
  • Janmashtami and Raksha Bandhan
  • Regional diversity in celebration
Week 9

Ethics, Values & Society

Living Dharma in society
  • Truthfulness and compassion
  • Respect for nature
  • Family and community responsibilities
  • Sewa (selfless service)
Week 10

Hindu Philosophical Schools

Exploring different schools of thought
  • Overview of major darshanas
  • Vedanta, Sankhya, and Yoga philosophy
  • Bhakti traditions
  • Diversity of interpretation
Week 11

Hindu Dharma in the Modern World

Contemporary relevance and global influence
  • Hindu communities worldwide
  • Yoga and meditation globally
  • Hindu contributions to philosophy, arts, and science
  • Misconceptions, stereotypes, and interfaith understanding
Week 12

Reflection, Integration & Celebration

Bringing the learning together
  • Review of key concepts
  • Personal reflections
  • Dharma in daily life
  • Continuing the learning journey
